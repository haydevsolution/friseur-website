CAREERS

ONE PROFESSION.
COUNTLESS OPPORTUNITIES.

Hairdressers are creative, enjoy their work and are an important part of the fashion and lifestyle industry. Once you have completed your training, you have a qualification for life — you can achieve anything with determination and hard work.

The possibilities are endless

  • Work in a salon as a stylist or colorist
  • Open your own salon, style backstage at fashion shows or work on film and photo productions
  • Become a trainer and inspire hairdressers around the world — or take on one of many roles in the industry

Best of all: through commitment you can become one of the best — earning great recognition and good money.

CAREER PROFILE 01

Hair & Make-up Artist

A hair and make-up artist specialises in hair styling and make-up — at fashion shows, for film, photo and TV productions or for companies in the industry. Clients include cosmetics and fashion companies, magazines and production firms. The job demands a high degree of flexibility and willingness to travel: to succeed, you should be able to show international work.

CAREER PROFILE 02

Trainer

A hairdressing trainer teaches future hairdressers or provides further training for journeymen. In the salon, the trainer is responsible for the apprentices’ education — until every skill for the journeyman examination is in place. You can also work as a trainer in industry, trade or at teaching institutes. You should enjoy teaching, explaining techniques and supporting the development of salon staff.

CAREER PROFILE 03

Hairdresser

Hairdressers are the creative all-rounders of the industry — no explicit specialisation, but strong in several areas: cutting, colouring and styling. They excel at consultation, know colour and hairstyle trends and master the latest techniques. Hair and beauty salons alike value these multi-talents.

CAREER PROFILE 04

Colorist

A colorist specialises in hair colour and colouring techniques — always up to date with trend shades, mastering the craft down to the last detail. Demand keeps growing, so more and more salons rely on at least one colorist in the team. You can also work for colour manufacturers: in their own studios, where colour concepts are created and tested on models.
